I conducted fundamental research and led to the design of an end-to-end prototype based on the use case of benefits, which was employees' common interest.

    Reflection

    What I've learned

    Design Handoff

    I started this project from scratch and this project was not shipped to development yet when I left. I finalized the wireframe design for the use case of benefits after many tweaks with a senior designer and the project manager and collected more data points for other use cases. I made the prototype in InVision and clearly communicate with the team who would keep on tweaking the design details after I left.

    Team Efforts Matter

    I strategically recruited 9 stakeholders and the other 10 ServiceNow employees. I worked closely with the Employee Experience team to develop research plans, conduct user research, validate ideation, and create prototypes.
    In this design, I mainly focused on information structure and wireframe design. If I have been given more time on research, I would work on refining the contents to be more personalized. I was also able to encounter ambiguities based on user interview findings, design systems, and usability research on best practices in order to make design decisions.

    Design Requires Inputs from Collaboration

    I learned the importance of collaboration among designers, researchers, engineers, and PMs, to make sure the work is user-friendly. I should also take product strategy and development feasibility into consideration.

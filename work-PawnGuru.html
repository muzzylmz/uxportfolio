    • PROBLEM

    How can we design the most effective mobile application to meet pawnshop owners' needs based on PawnGuru's existing website?

    Pawnshops are often the most immediate source of funds for low-income/underbanked people.

    Pawnguru, an Ann Arbor-based startup, is a web-based platform that connects users to various pawnshops in their local areas. Customers can fill out some info about the item, including type, condition, and a good photo. From there, local pawnshops will get notifications and they can decide if they want to make an offer on the item or ask the customer to bring the item to the shop to make an in-person deal.

    Through studying client's insights and business problems, we created a mobile app that should adopt the current functions of browsing items, connecting with customers, making offers, and marking closed deals on the PawnGuru web-based platform, which could benefit their users (Pawnshop owners) and increase overall company growth.

    Research

    Interviews

    To understand how pawnshop owners utilize PawnGuruâs online platform, my team conducting semi-structured interviews with 4 pawnshop owners who were users of the Pawnguru desktop platform. I visited two pawnshops located in Dearborn(Michigan) and Brighton(Michigan) and along with two on-call interviews with users in Florida and Georgia. From the interviewees, I learned that pawnshop owners' pain points include:

    1. Pawnshop owners rarely close their deals strictly online

    I always ask my customers to come in and show me the item before I can pay them.

    When owners find an item they are interested in buying, they often request that the customer bring the item to the shop. Owners have also expressed frustration that it is difficult to know the quality of an item with only a sparse description and that customers often fail to provide them detailed information or photos of the item.

    2. Pawnshop owners send repetitive messages to customers

    It's time-consuming that I have to message every customer to provide me more details about the item.

    The messages that pawnshop owners send to customers are usually along the lines of âCan you bring the item to our shop?â or âCan you give me more details about the item?â. The content of the messages usually do not stray away from these, but currently, owners type these repetitive messages out for every item that they are interested in buying.

    3. Pawnshop owners are only interested in select categories of items

    I mostly deal with guns and do not want to see messages related to other items such as handbags that I'm not interested in on the website of PawnGuru.

    Some pawnshop owners would always buy handbags, while others are unwilling to deal with them at all. Different stores specialize in different types of items.

    Through interviews, I had a good understanding of the current user experience of the PawnGuru's website and how pawnshop owners would interact with a mobile application. I wanted to design an intuitive mobile experience facing pawnshop owners to improve their customer engagement experience in finding, connecting with, and increasing closed deals with their customers will be the ultimate goal.Â

    Refined Workflow Diagram for a Mobile App

    Based on findings on workflow analysis, I decided to modify the 3 workflows (Browse, Chat, and Retail) to be more user-friendly and used the diagrams to visualize how pawnshop owners can navigate the PawnGuru platform.

    1. Browse Workflow (where pawnshop owners can see the items their customers posted)


    Findings: Pawnshop owners rarely close their deals strictly online. They often request customers to bring the item to the shop or ask customers to provide more details about the item to confirm the itemâs quality before offering a deal.


    Design Decisions:
    1. Pawnshop can see all the items their potential customers post on the platform.
    2. We kept PawnGuruâs âNot Interestedâ feature for the items so that owners can choose to ignore the requests that they are not interested in.
    3. Once an item is selected, pawnshop owner has two options of âMaking an offerâ and âContactâ the customer.
    4. We implemented more functions of interacting with potential customers with the original "Contact" function" provide a list of contact options.



    2. Retail Workflow (where pawnshop owners receive a notification when customers send requests for items)


    Findings: Effective customer-owner interactions are essential in successfully closing a deal. It is important that both customers and owners be responsive. A good, clear conversation with real-time notifications can improve efficiency.


    Design Decisions:
    1. If owners are interested in selling the item to the customer, they may contact the customer directly.
    2. Owners also have the option of directly making a phone call to the customer, as well as removing a request from their âRetailâ page if not interested.
    3. Once an item is selected, pawnshop owner has two options of âMaking an offerâ and âContactâ the customer.
    4. We assumed customers would like to know more details about an item before buying it. We decided to add an option where pawnshop owners can upload a picture of the item for the customer to see.



    3. Chat Workflow (where pawnshop owners and customers can communicate over the details of the item)


    Findings: Pawnshop owners spend extra time typing the same messages to customers.     REFINE

    Iterations to improve design

    My Design Decisions on Messaging

    I was mainly responsible for designing the messaging flow. When designing a mobile app for pawnshop owners, I spent lots of time thinking about how to allow pawnshop owners to distinguish where the conversation is initiated from and how to make pawnshop owners have effective conversations with customers. Therefore, I would like to discuss the design decisions I made for the messaging features.

    One-stop place to filter messages

    The conversations can be filtered based on whether it is initiated from the Browse or Retail feature. The pawnshop owners also have the option to view inactive chats, which are conversations in which the item of interest is no longer available to buy because a deal has already been made between the customer and another pawnshop owner.

    Quick access to make an offer and report a closed deal

    To save pawnshop owners' typing time, I provided a list of pre-made messages for pawnshop owners to select.

    âMake an offerâ and âMade a dealâ are features I introduced in the message feature, as those are goals the owners may want to complete while in conversation with a customer. When the pawnshop owners select âMade a deal,â they are reporting to PawnGuru when the deal is made, how much the deal is made for, and the type of deal that is closed.

    FINAL DESIGN

    Key Features

    Browse Feature

    Allows pawnshop owners can see the items their potential customers posted and wanted to sell/pawn.


    The browse feature supports quick âContactâ and âNot Interestedâ buttons for the individual items, as well as an advanced filter. The user can filter by category and distance, and view the items they have auto-responded to.

    Our user research showed that pawnshop owners always contact customers for more information on the items they are seeing on their browse page. We provide a list of options to contact the customer.

    Messaging Feature

    Allows pawnshop owners to view a list of items and continue conversations with customers.

    The conversations can be filtered based on whether they are initiated from the Browse or Retail tabs. Pawnshop owners also have the option to view inactive chats, which are conversations in which the item of interest is no longer available to buy.

    Request Feature

    Allows pawnshop owners to receive a notification when customers send requests for items they are interested in buying.

    Pawnshop owners can delete a request from their page by swiping left and can begin giving more information to the customer by selecting the item.
    They can send auto or customized messages to the customer with a photo.
